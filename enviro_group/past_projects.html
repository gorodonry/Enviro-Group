2020

9th August

Working Bee

On Sunday 9th August we held a working bee as part of our forest restoration project. The aim of this project was to plant fast-growing native species that will create a canopy to shade out the weeds. We will come back again later and plant slow-growing, proper native forest once the threat from weeds have gone. 27 people turned up - students and their families, with one or two teachers. We estimate around 500 trees were planted, which is great. Thank you to all those who helped out, both organising and attending. Keep an eye on our website - we're thinking of holding another one shortly (possibly a weeding one this time, to protect the trees already planted!).
