About Us

Welcome to the Onslow Enviro Group webpage!

We are all about the promotion of environmental sustainability within our community. This is key to a positive, functional environment - one that isn't being swamped by rubbish or climate change.

The Onslow Enviro Group brings together students and staff who are concerned about issues impacting our environment and who are committed to promoting positive change in our community. We work together to promote our aims through advocacy, fundraising and school projects. We facilitate inclusive practice and promote the development of student leaders with our various projects each having students overseeing the work.

Check out our blog below for recent and upcoming activity. Our latest goal is the reintroduction of native birds and lizards to Onslow and the renovation of Table Mountain for the purpose of providing sheltered places for plants, animals, and people on Table Mountain.

Let your friends know about us - the more people we get on board the better the environment will be in the years to come.

Election Promises

Posted on 13th September 2020 @10:23am

Informative and unbiased page on environmental party policies for the upcoming election. Of note, the Greens are the only major party with a comprehensive and sensible environmental outlook. A trend I noticed was that the further towards the right of the political spectrum a party is, the more environmentally harmful it seems to get. Link to the website can be found here.

Although most of you won't be able to vote in the election, I though it would be cool for you to understand NZ's party's policies towards the environment. This will help with making an informed decision when you are eligible to vote (if not already) and provide you with some insight into the immediate future depending on who gets elected.

Working Bee

Posted on 12th August 2020 @5:17pm

Thank you to all those who attended last Sunday's working bee. Between us we planted up about 500 trees around Waipahihi. Awesome effort - Make sure to come back in 20 years and see the resultant forest!

However, with planting comes weeding, a necessary evil to ensure the survival of our plantation. We plan on recruiting volunteers to spend half an hour every fortnight or so to weed the revegetated area. Email enviro@onslow.school.nz if you are interested in helping out with this.

Plant of the Season

Posted on 27th June 2020 @2:15pm

Otari Wilton Bush Trust's plant of the season for this winter is ranunculus gracilipes (no common name). For those of you who don't know, 'montane' forest is forest found between 300 and 800 metres above sea level.


Ranunculus gracilipes

This is a plant of higher montane to subalpine grassland and herbfield, found in the South and Stewart Islands. I collected the illustrated specimen (as seed) two years ago from the Remarkables, as Chaerophyllum colensoi, and it happily masqueraded as C. colensoi in the nursery until recently when it flowered.

Drawing and blurb credit: Eleanor Burton.
